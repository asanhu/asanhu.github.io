---
title: "Book Review: Never Let Me Go"
date: 2025-07-06
tags: [books]
---

One of the saddest, most helpless and hopeless books I've ever read.

I was drawn in by the quiet, almost gentle narration from the perspective of a cloned girl. At first, I didn't even realize she was cloned, and that's precisely when the heartbreak hit. The moment it clicked, the sadness came flooding in.

We're shown a world where human beings with full emotional capacity are created solely to serve others: to cure "real" humans, the ones deemed more worthy simply because they were born the natural way. Who gave us the right to create life only to take it away for our own survival? To extend our lives with better organs, at the cost of another's existence?

What struck me most is how the society in the novel, much like ours, copes with this horror: by turning a blind eye. By convincing ourselves these beings are somehow less than human, unworthy of decent treatment, education, or even livable conditions. It's chilling how clever and how self-deceiving we can be.

What does it mean to be human? Who gets to decide that? And who gets to determine whose life is more valuable?
