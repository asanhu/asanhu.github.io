Some cities announce themselves; Kyoto lets you find it. I spent three days
mostly walking — Philosopher's Path in the early morning before the crowds,
a quiet temple whose name I never learned, and the best bowl of udon I've had,
eaten standing next to a vending machine.

A few notes for future me:

Go early or go late. Between 10am and 4pm, the famous spots belong to tour
groups. Before 8am they belong to you and the cats.

The best moments weren't on the itinerary. A stranger's recommendation, a
wrong turn into a pottery district, an hour watching the Kamo River do nothing
in particular.
