---
title: On Unfinished Things
date: 2026-07-10
tags: [epiphanies]
---

It hit me while abandoning a book halfway through, guilt-free, for the first
time in my life: finishing things is not a virtue in itself.

We treat completion as proof of character. But a book that stopped teaching
you, a project that no longer fits, a habit that served an older version of
you — carrying these to the finish line isn't discipline, it's sunk-cost
dressed up as integrity.

The better question isn't "did I finish it?" but "did I get what I came for?"
