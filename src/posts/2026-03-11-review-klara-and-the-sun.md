---
title: "Klara and the Sun"
date: 2026-03-11
tags: [books]
---

This one left me with a very mixed and unsettled feeling, maybe that's why this review came almost 4 months after I finished it. After reading Never Let Me Go, my expectations for Ishiguro's more recent work were high, but this book just didn't strike me in the same way. Throughout, I found myself caught between appreciating the keenness of observation and the emotional depth that an AI companion could offer, and yet puzzled that a robot of such intelligence could so fundamentally misread how humans actually work. One plot point that puzzled me in particular: Klara going to pray to the Sun for her owner's health, it just felt genuinely bizarre.

I think the premise had so much more potential, especially coming from Ishiguro. But the story struggles to escape the clichés of human-robot intimacy and never quite reaches the depth I was hoping for.

What bothers me most, perhaps, is the overly childlike narrative tone, at times it felt less like literary fiction and more like a book written for children.
