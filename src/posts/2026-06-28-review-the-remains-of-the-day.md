---
title: "Book Review: The Remains of the Day"
date: 2026-06-28
tags: [books]
---

Ishiguro writes an entire tragedy without ever raising his voice. Stevens, the
butler narrator, spends the novel explaining why his life was well spent, and
the explaining itself is the evidence that it wasn't.

What stayed with me is how the book handles regret: not as a dramatic reckoning
but as something glimpsed sideways, in the gap between what Stevens says and
what he clearly feels.

**Verdict:** read it slowly. 5/5.
