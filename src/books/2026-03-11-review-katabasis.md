My very last R.F. Kuang book (before she publishes anything new), and this one is even wilder and more far-out than anything else from her exceptional imagination: a magic grad student accidentally (or not so) kills her advisor and descends into hell to bring him back. The entire story surprisingly never felt strange or forced; the way she describes the different houses of hell echoes remarkably with ancient Chinese folklore and mythology, deeply rooted in Chinese philosophy. Whenever I read a Kuang book, I always feel that strong sense of familiarity, even when the story couldn't be more remote from my own reality. Her ability to weave Asian philosophy and ancient Chinese context into what appears on the surface to be a thoroughly Westernized story is endlessly fascinating.

This is also the first of her books to end with a genuinely happy ending, and I read it with a great sense of lightness.

What a fun read!
