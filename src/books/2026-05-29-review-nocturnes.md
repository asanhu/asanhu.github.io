Wrapped up another one by Kazuo Ishiguro. In this collection, he continues with his usual touch of wit and sadness. These five stories all feature a young musician with great potential, but they are somehow buried in the hustle of life and haven't yet risen to their prime.

These stories made me feel a bit lost sometimes: all of the plots end right after the young musician's interactions with a virtuoso. They just stop there without revealing what happens next to the protagonist, or whether they were truly inspired to live up to their potential. Each short piece could've easily been extended into a complete novel of its own, as there are so many side stories alongside the main plot that could be developed. But maybe that's exactly why he wrote them like this :)
