I've really enjoyed picking up random books from bookstores while I travel. As long as the book is readable, I try to finish it, and most of them have turned out to be better than I expected so far.

Frankie was one of those happy surprises. It's a historical fiction novel that I read in just six hours, and it introduced me to the Irish writer Graham Norton (who's also a famous TV host but I didn't know that before).

The book is easy to read and quite fun. Even though some of the storylines are a bit predictable, there are still a few unexpected twists. What really amazed me was how well Norton writes about women, their thoughts, feelings, and struggles. He does it with so much care and detail that I actually stopped and thought, how does he understand all this so well as a man?

The story follows Frankie, an Irish girl who loses her parents and ends up married at 18 to a man twice her age. Her life takes many turns: she gets left behind, moves to London, then ends up in New York by accident and stays there for 20 years. She builds a new life, starts a career, falls in love, and then loses that love when fame changes everything. Her husband cheats on her just when they finally "make it," and that part felt especially heartbreaking.

What I loved most is how the story is told from Frankie's point of view as an older woman, talking to her carer. Even though she's been through a lot, she tells her story with humor and kindness. It makes the book feel warm and full of life, even with all the sad parts.
