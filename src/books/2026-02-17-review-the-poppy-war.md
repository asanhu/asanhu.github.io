3 books with 1,800 pages in 3 months... wow….

I spent at least 30 mins every night devouring the R.F. Kuang debut trilogy (and never get tired of it!). Every time I picked them up, a pragmatic voice in my head asked, "Why am I reading this?" But I shattered the question immediately because I was having so much fun!

Reading The Poppy War was a disorienting experience for me that a Western reader might not resonate with. Kuang uses names deeply etched into Chinese history and mythology—Su Daji, Jiang Ziya, Nezha. These are national legends from Gods of Honour (with 20+ TV/film adaptations), but she remixes them into a completely new narrative.

I found myself constantly battling my "stereotypical impressions." I know who Su Daji and Nezha are supposed to be. I know the story of "Jinzhou." But in Kuang's world, they have new identities and destinies. It is a weird, fascinating combo: the comfort of familiarity clashing with the shock of a completely new fictional war.

About the ending: If you've read R.F. Kuang, you know she doesn't believe in happy endings. She doesn't try to please the audience at all (but that's why I love her!). The trilogy ends with sacrifice and forces you to confront a heavy question: Can a single hero save a nation? Or does the hero have to burn so the nation can survive?

Verdict: 10/10. ⭐️⭐️⭐️⭐️⭐️
If you haven't read her yet, start with Yellowface, then you will be as obsessed as I am! And I was so happy learning she released a new book last year, so I still have one more massive book from her to devour!
